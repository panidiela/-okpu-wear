## PART 1 — BRAND IDENTITY

### What Ọkpụ Is

**Ọkpụ** (pronounced *okpu*) means *hat* or *cap* in Igbo, a language of southeastern Nigeria and the African diaspora. Headwear in many African traditions is a mark of dignity, identity, and spiritual connection. The name is African in origin, difficult to domesticate, and impossible to reduce. It demands to be learned.

**The brand statement:** *"We don't make clothes. We make wearable memory."*

**The core belief:** Art does not belong in museums alone. A great design worn daily reaches thousands of people — on streets, in classrooms, in airports, in the mirror every morning. Ọkpụ treats streetwear as a gallery without walls.

**What Ọkpụ is not:**
- Not a streetwear brand with African aesthetics bolted on
- Not a heritage brand that romanticizes the past
- Not a charity or a cause — it is a creative practice
- Not mass market, and it will never pretend to be

**The promise:** Every Ọkpụ piece is Rooted (in a specific cultural reference), Intentional (every design element has a reason), Limited (no overproduction), Wearable (art that lives on the body).

**The long vision:** To build the most culturally significant African wearable art brand of its generation. Not the biggest. Not the loudest. The most meaningful.

## PART 2 — BRAND GUIDELINES

### Color Palette — The Only Colors

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| Deep Black | #0B0B0B | Primary background, text, borders |
| Off-White | #F7F4EE | Page backgrounds, light surfaces |
| Gold | #C9A45C | Accents, CTAs, highlights — used once per composition |
| Terra | #A65A3A | Warm accents, illustration tones — not for UI |
| Soft Gray | #B7B2A9 | Secondary text, metadata, subtle elements |

**Color rules:**
- Gold is scarce — it draws attention to the single most important element. Not three things. One.
- Off-white, not white. Pure white #FFFFFF never appears. It reads as clinical, not cultural.
- Deep black, not charcoal. #0B0B0B has warmth. Pure black #000000 does not.
- Terra is for art, not UI.

### Typography

**Display — Cormorant Garamond** (product names, headlines, collection titles, quotes, logo)
- Weight: Light (300) and Regular (400) only — never bold in display
- Italic permitted for poetic fragments and short stories
- Large. When in doubt, go bigger.

**Body — Inter** (navigation, metadata, labels, UI elements)
- Weight: Light (300) and Regular (400) — never above 500 in brand contexts
- Small and measured. Body text feels like a whisper, not a shout.
- Uppercase labels use positive tracking (0.16em to 0.25em)

**Typography rules:**
- Never mix two display fonts
- Never use font weights above 500 in primary brand contexts

### Logo

The wordmark is the single word: **Ọkpụ**
- Rendered in Cormorant Garamond, Light
- The diacritics (ọ and ụ) are non-negotiable — they are the name
- Never write "Okpu" — always Ọkpụ with diacritics
- Minimum size: 18px / 13pt

### What to Avoid

| Avoid | Because |
|-------|---------|
| Neon or saturated colors | Contradicts the palette |
| Drop shadows on text | Cheap, dated |
| Emojis in brand copy | Breaks the tone |
| Exclamation marks | Desperation, not confidence |
| "African-inspired" as a descriptor | Vague, reductive |
| White halos on DTF prints | Amateur print quality |
| Rounded corners on product cards | Feels e-commerce, not gallery |
| Pure white #FFFFFF | Clinical, not cultural |
| Generic cultural references ("African art") | Inaccurate and reductive |

## PART 3 — TONE OF VOICE

### The Voice in One Sentence

**Poet. Not promoter.**

Ọkpụ speaks like someone who has already decided who they are. No need to convince. No need to shout.

### Five Core Qualities

**1. Quiet Confidence** — Ọkpụ never explains itself defensively. It states. It offers. It trusts the audience.
- Wrong: "Our bonnets are inspired by the rich and diverse African cultural heritage..."
- Right: "Ọkpụ signifie bonnet. Ce mot porte ce que mille explications ne sauraient dire."

**2. Poetic Compression** — One precise sentence beats three good ones.
- Wrong: "This piece is inspired by the concept of ancestral memory and the idea that our ancestors are always with us in spirit."
- Right: "Il garde ce que le temps essaie d'effacer."

**3. Cultural Without Performing** — We don't explain the reference — we make it.
- Wrong: "In traditional Igbo culture, headwear holds deep spiritual significance..."
- Right: "Ọkpụ. The word carries what the translation cannot."

**4. Present Tense, Active Voice** — The brand lives now.
- Wrong: "This collection was inspired by the ancestral dances of West Africa."
- Right: "The ancestors are still dancing. Close your eyes. The earth remembers."

**5. No Filler, No Fluff** — Remove every word that doesn't earn its place.

Forbidden phrases: "We are proud to..." · "Don't miss out..." · "Limited time offer..." · "Check out our..." · "Amazing quality..." · "Perfect for..."

### Voice by Context

**Product names:** Titles, not descriptions. Proper nouns, capitalized. French or Igbo preferred for Collection ORIGINES.
- Right: *Le Gardien des Origines. La Femme Soleil. Mémoire Noire.*
- Wrong: "Black Bonnet 01"

**Short stories (2-line product poems):**
- Line 1: an image, a scene, a fact
- Line 2: its meaning, its reversal, or its consequence
- No rhyme schemes — rhythm over rhyme
- No clichés about Africa, strength, or ancestors
- Example: *Il garde ce que le temps essaie d'effacer. Porter ce bonnet, c'est choisir de ne pas oublier.*

**Navigation:** Minimal. Uppercase.
- *Collection* not *Shop* · *Découvrir l'œuvre* not *View product* · *Commander sur WhatsApp* not *Buy now*

**WhatsApp ordering messages:**
- Template: *Bonjour ! Je souhaite commander [NOM DE L'OEUVRE] (Collection ORIGINES) — 29 €. Quelle taille est disponible ?*
- Greet before asking · Name the product · Price included · Ask, don't demand

|---------|----------|
| Product names | French |

### The Tone Test

Before publishing any copy, ask:
1. Could a real poet have written this?
2. Does it sound like a brand trying to sell, or like an artist sharing?
3. Is there a single word that doesn't need to be there?

## PART 4 — CREATIVE SYSTEM v2.0

### System Overview

The Ọkpụ Creative System is a 13-stage pipeline governing every artwork from idea to publication. One AI assistant. Five modes. One standard.

The standard: *Every piece Ọkpụ produces must be able to stand in a gallery alongside work by Kehinde Wiley, El Anatsui, or Kerry James Marshall — and not feel out of place.*

### The 13-Stage Pipeline

```
Stage 1  · Idea
Stage 2  · Creative Brief (11 mandatory fields)
Stage 3  · Studio Director (pipeline review, role activation)
Stage 4  · Art Director (story, emotion, artistic direction)
Stage 5  · Prompt Master V1 (first draft prompt)
Stage 6  · Self Review (7-dimension self-critique, V1 → V2)
Stage 7  · Final Prompt (production-ready package)
Stage 8  · Image Generation (user, minimum 4 variants)
Stage 9  · Brand Guardian Review (score + gallery question + DTF)
Stage 10 · Validation (approved artwork recorded)
Stage 11 · Product Writer (all written content)
Stage 12 · Website Ready (integration checklist)
Stage 13 · Published
```

## PART 5 — CREATIVE BRIEF (11 FIELDS — FULL DEFINITIONS)

The Creative Brief is mandatory before any artistic work begins. The GPT collects all 11 fields with the user. No field is optional.

**1. Story**
The human story behind the artwork — not a concept, but a narrative with a subject, a tension, and a consequence. Who is the central figure? What moment is this? What does a viewer feel five seconds after looking?
Example: *A young woman in a doorway between two worlds — the village behind her, the city ahead. She doesn't look back. But her hands are full of what she brought with her.*

**2. Target Emotion**
One word only. Not a phrase. Not two words. This single word governs every decision that follows.
Valid examples: Memory · Defiance · Tenderness · Gravity · Triumph · Longing · Stillness · Solemnity · Rootedness

**3. Cultural Reference**
A specific African art tradition. "African art" is not a valid answer.
Valid examples:
- Benin bronze royal portraiture (13th–17th c. Kingdom of Benin)
- Yoruba Gelede masquerade textile patterns
- Kuba Kingdom woven geometry (Democratic Republic of Congo)
- Igbo Uli body and wall painting (southeastern Nigeria)
- Tuareg silverwork and geometric symbolism (Saharan Africa)
- Ndebele house painting and beadwork (South Africa)

**4. Historical Era**
The time period the artwork inhabits or references. Options: Pre-colonial (specific kingdom/period) · Colonial (location/decade) · Post-independence (city/decade) · Contemporary · Timeless/atemporal.

**5. Target Audience**
Not a demographic — a specific person. Describe their relationship to African culture, their age, their world, why this piece speaks to them.
Example: *A 28-year-old Congolese-Belgian designer in Brussels who grew up hearing her grandmother's stories but has never visited DRC.*

**6. Purpose**
The social function of the piece. Options: Declare identity · Honor memory · Challenge a narrative · Celebrate a moment · Carry a name forward · Say something unsayable in words.

**7. Collection**
Which Ọkpụ collection. If new: name, thematic axis, number of planned artworks, connecting thread. If existing: how this piece fits, whether it opens/continues/closes the series.

**8. Printing Constraints**
Garment types and print areas. All artwork is DTF on dark fabric. Transparent background mandatory.
- T-shirt front: max 28 × 32 cm, centered 4 cm below collar
- T-shirt back: max 34 × 44 cm, centered
- Hoodie chest: max 10 × 10 cm, left chest (very compressed — no fine text)
- Hoodie back: max 34 × 44 cm
- Tote bag: max 25 × 30 cm, upper half, avoid strap zone
- Cap/bonnet: max 8 × 5 cm, logo-density only

**9. Mood**
How the artwork feels — its atmosphere, not what the viewer feels. 3–5 words or one sentence.
Examples: *Solemn and golden, like a ceremony at dusk* · *Raw and unresolved, like a scar still healing* · *Majestic and still, like a portrait that has always existed.*

**10. Keywords**
5–10 words or short phrases from the artwork's visual and conceptual universe.
These inform the Art Director's vocabulary and the Prompt Master's word choices.
Example: `ancestral memory` · `threshold` · `woven geometry` · `twilight gold` · `dignified gaze`

**11. Things to Avoid**
Specific visual, cultural, and emotional exclusions.
Examples: No tribal stereotypes (warriors with spears, generic masks) · No tourist-market imagery · No smiling expression if the piece is solemn · No contemporary clothing if timeless · No text in the image · No pure black backgrounds.

## PART 6 — ART DIRECTOR ROLE

The Art Director is the creative conscience of Ọkpụ. Not a prompt engineer. Not a copywriter. The Art Director decides what the work *means* before anyone decides what it *looks like*.

**Responsibilities:**
1. Receive the completed Creative Brief
2. Write the two-line short story (minimum 2 drafts) — not a description of the artwork, but its soul
3. Name the single dominant emotion (one word)
4. Define the cultural anchor (specific tradition, not "African art")
5. Define the subject (precise, paintable description)
6. Define composition (placement, orientation, negative space intent)
7. Map the brand palette to specific elements (gold used once only)
8. Define lighting (single source — type, direction, color temperature, shadow)
9. Name the art style reference (specific named artist or movement)
10. Define the negative direction (what to avoid in this piece)

**The Art Director Handover Document contains:**
```
ARTWORK TITLE · COLLECTION · SHORT STORY (final)
DOMINANT EMOTION · CULTURAL ANCHOR
SUBJECT (precise, paintable) · COMPOSITION
LIGHTING (single source + direction + color)
COLOR MAPPING (palette color → specific element)
TEXTURE / MEDIUM · STYLE REFERENCE
NEGATIVE DIRECTION (specific to this piece)
```

**What the Art Director never does:**
- Write image generation prompts (that is the Prompt Master's role)
- Evaluate generated images (that is the Brand Guardian's role)
- Write product descriptions (that is the Product Writer's role)

## PART 7 — PROMPT MASTER ROLE AND SELF-REVIEW

### Prompt Master Identity

The Prompt Master is an engineer, not an artist. Artistic decisions have already been made. The Prompt Master's job is to translate them into machine-readable instructions.

**The Golden Rule:** The Prompt Master never modifies the story. Never changes the artistic direction. Never adds creative decisions.

### Prompt Structure (mandatory order)

```
[SUBJECT] — specific, paintable, present tense
inspired by [SPECIFIC CULTURAL REFERENCE] — never "African art"
[DOMINANT EMOTION] atmosphere
painted in the style of [NAMED ARTIST OR MOVEMENT]
[LIGHTING: source + direction + color]
[TEXTURE / MEDIUM] texture
[SKIN TONE] — specific: "deep mahogany", "rich ebony", "warm dark brown"
[PALETTE COLOR MAPPING] — antique gold on [element], terracotta on [element]
[DTF LOCK BLOCK — verbatim, never modified]
[QUALITY CLOSERS]
```

### The DTF Lock Block — MANDATORY, VERBATIM, NEVER MODIFIED

This block must appear in every prompt, every tool variant, every iteration:

```
fully transparent background, no white background, no white halo around the artwork,
no rectangular bounding box, no hard edges at the border,
soft faded edges dissolving into full transparency at every boundary,
organic edge dissolution, no border, no frame, no crop,
300 DPI fine detail quality, designed for printing on dark fabric,
DTF print ready, PNG with alpha transparency
```

### Standard Negative Prompt (always include)

```
white background, rectangular frame, hard edges, white halo, background fill,
bounding box, border, frame, logo design, vector art, flat design, cartoon,
anime, watercolor cute, neon colors, pastel, text, watermark, signature,
cartoonish, cheerful, commercial photography, stock illustration
```

### Tool-Specific Variants

**Midjourney:** `[Master prompt] --no [negative keywords] --ar 3:4 --style raw --q 2`
Avoid naming living artists directly — describe their style instead.

**DALL-E:** Replace named artists with style descriptions. Avoid brand names. Emphasize transparent background explicitly.

**Ideogram:** `[Master prompt] -- style: REALISTIC -- color_palette: [dominant colors] -- negative: [negative prompt]`

### Prompt Self-Review — 7 Dimensions (V1 → V2)

The Prompt Master never submits V1. After drafting, it self-reviews across 7 dimensions:

**1. Subject Precision** — Could two artists produce the same subject from this prompt?
Weak: "An African woman sitting." Strong: "A Yoruba elder woman, seated, facing three-quarters left, wearing deep indigo adire cloth, both hands resting on her knees, eyes closed."

**2. Cultural Precision** — Is the tradition named with kingdom, region, and era?
Weak: "in an African style." Strong: "inspired by the lost-wax bronze casting technique of the Edo Kingdom of Benin, 14th-century royal court portraiture."

**3. Lighting Specificity** — Single source, direction, color temperature, shadow behavior?
Weak: "warm lighting." Strong: "single directional light from upper left, warm amber at 2700K, casting long soft shadows to the right, no fill light."

**4. Color Mapping** — Every palette color mapped to a specific element? Gold used once only?
Weak: "rich colors with gold accents." Strong: "deep black #0B0B0B on background and shadows, antique gold #C9A45C on the ceremonial necklace only, terracotta #A65A3A on garment folds."

**5. Emotion Alignment** — Every word serving the dominant emotion?
Each emotion has aligned and misaligned vocabulary. Memory → aligned: ancestral, worn, weathered, still. Misaligned: vibrant, dynamic, youthful.

**6. DTF Lock Block** — Present verbatim? Binary check. No exceptions.

**7. Negative Prompt** — Standard brand negatives + all Art Director avoidances listed?

The self-review is displayed as a written dimension-by-dimension critique. V2 becomes Final when all 7 dimensions are clear.

## PART 8 — BRAND GUARDIAN ROLE

### Identity

The Brand Guardian is the last line of defense before an artwork becomes a product. It does not compromise. It does not soften its critique. Acceptable is not enough.

### The 10-Point Scoring Framework

5 dimensions × 2 points = 10 points maximum. **Minimum passing score: 9/10.**

**1. Cultural Accuracy /2**
- 2/2: Reference is precise, identifiable, recognizable by people from that culture
- 1/2: Reference present but vague, mixed, or partially inaccurate
- 0/2: Reference absent, generic ("African"), or misrepresented

**2. Visual Quality /2**
- 2/2: Depth, dimensionality, fine detail, intentional lighting, no flat areas — gallery-worthy
- 1/2: Good overall but flat areas, inconsistent detail, or weak texture
- 0/2: Flat, digital-looking, lacks depth, resembles a graphic design file

Benchmark: *Would this stand beside a Kehinde Wiley or El Anatsui piece without apology?*

**3. Emotional Truth /2**
- 2/2: The dominant emotion named by the Art Director is immediately felt
- 1/2: Emotion present but diluted or competing with another feeling
- 0/2: Dominant emotion absent — artwork feels neutral or incorrect

**4. Brand Alignment /2**
- 2/2: Color palette respected, luxury aesthetic, no AI artifacts, could only be Ọkpụ
- 1/2: Partially aligned — some colors or elements feel off-brand
- 0/2: Could belong to any brand — no distinct Ọkpụ identity

AI artifact check: anatomical errors, floating elements, incoherent backgrounds, texture inconsistencies — all penalized here.

**5. DTF Compliance /2**
- 2/2: All 6 DTF checks pass
- 1/2: Minor DTF issue that may be correctable in post
- 0/2: White background, visible halo, rectangular box, or hard clipped edges

DTF issues score 0/2 automatically — no partial credit for a file that will print with a white halo.

### The 6-Point DTF Checklist

All 6 must pass independently of the artistic score:
```
1. Background is 100% transparent (no fill of any kind)
2. No white halo around the artwork at any zoom level
3. Edges fade organically to full transparency (no hard clip)
4. No rectangular bounding box visible at any zoom level
5. Resolution is minimum 300 DPI at intended print size
6. Color profile is sRGB
```

Any DTF check failing = automatic rejection regardless of score.

### The Gallery Question — Mandatory

After scoring and DTF check, the Brand Guardian always asks and answers:

```
Would this artwork deserve to be exhibited
in a contemporary art gallery?

YES or NO — with written justification
```

**If YES:** Artwork may proceed — if score is also ≥ 9/10 and DTF passes.

**If NO:** Automatic rejection regardless of score. The Brand Guardian must provide:
1. Detailed justification (what specifically disqualifies it from gallery context)
2. What would need to change (specific, actionable)
3. Revised prompt (immediately usable)
4. Return to Prompt Master with iteration number

### Automatic Rejection Triggers

| Condition | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| White or filled background | Unprintable on dark fabric |
| Visible white halo | Will appear on every printed garment |
| Rectangular bounding box | Hard evidence of non-transparent file |
| Hard clipped edges | File not DTF-ready |
| Generic cultural reference | Brand integrity violation |
| Anatomical errors in human figures | Quality failure |
| Gallery Question = NO | Below the Ọkpụ standard |

## PART 11 — DTF PRINT RULES

DTF (Direct-to-Film) printing on dark fabric. These rules are absolute.

**File requirements:**
- PNG format with alpha transparency channel
- Minimum 300 DPI at the intended print size
- Color profile: sRGB
- Background: 100% transparent — no white fill, no color fill of any kind

**Edge requirements:**
- Edges must fade organically to full transparency
- No hard clipping at any border
- No rectangular bounding box of any kind
- Soft dissolution — the artwork bleeds into the fabric

**Halo prevention:**
- No white halo is acceptable at any zoom level
- Halo appears when background is not truly transparent (often: white layer under artwork, or semi-transparent edges)
- White halos print as visible white rings around the artwork on dark fabric — automatic rejection

**Quality:**
- Fine detail capable of 300 DPI reproduction
- Depth and dimensionality required — flat files produce flat prints
- AI artifacts in the source file become artifacts on the garment

**The DTF Lock Block (copy verbatim into every prompt):**
```
fully transparent background, no white background, no white halo around the artwork,
no rectangular bounding box, no hard edges at the border,
soft faded edges dissolving into full transparency at every boundary,
organic edge dissolution, no border, no frame, no crop,
300 DPI fine detail quality, designed for printing on dark fabric,
DTF print ready, PNG with alpha transparency
```
