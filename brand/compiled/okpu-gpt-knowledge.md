## PART 1 — BRAND IDENTITY

**Ọkpụ** (pronounced *okpu*) is inspired by a word from the Igbo language evoking *the sculptor* — the one who reveals a work by giving form to matter. The sculptor does not merely shape wood, stone, or bronze. He gives visible form to the beliefs, emotions, stories, and memory of a people.

**Brand statement:** *"We don't make clothes. We make wearable memory."*

**What Ọkpụ is not:**
- Not a streetwear brand with African aesthetics bolted on
- Not a heritage brand that romanticizes the past
- Not a charity or cause — it is a creative practice
- Not mass market

**The promise — every Ọkpụ piece is:**
- **Rooted** — specific cultural reference, never vague Afro-aesthetic
- **Intentional** — every design element has a reason
- **Limited** — no overproduction
- **Wearable** — art that lives on the body

**The long vision:** To build the most culturally significant African wearable art brand of its generation. Not the biggest. Not the loudest. The most meaningful.

## PART 2 — BRAND GUIDELINES

### Color Palette

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| Deep Black | #0B0B0B | Primary background, text, borders |
| Off-White | #F7F4EE | Page backgrounds, light surfaces |
| Gold | #C9A45C | Accents, CTAs, highlights — used once per composition |
| Terra | #A65A3A | Warm accents, illustration tones — not for UI |
| Soft Gray | #B7B2A9 | Secondary text, metadata, subtle elements |

**Rules:** Gold is scarce — it draws attention to one element only. Never pure white #FFFFFF (clinical). Never pure black #000000 (cold). Terra is for art, not UI.

### Typography

**Display — Cormorant Garamond:** Product names, headlines, collection titles, quotes, logo. Weight: Light (300) and Regular (400) only. Large. Never bold in display.

**Body — Inter:** Navigation, metadata, labels, UI. Weight: Light (300) and Regular (400). Never above 500 in brand contexts. Uppercase labels: tracking 0.16em–0.25em.

### Logo

The wordmark is the single word: **Ọkpụ** — rendered in Cormorant Garamond, Light. The diacritics (ọ and ụ) are non-negotiable. Never write "Okpu."

### What to Avoid

Neon or saturated colors · Drop shadows on text · Emojis in brand copy · Exclamation marks · "African-inspired" as a descriptor · White halos on DTF prints · Pure white #FFFFFF · Generic cultural references ("African art")

## PART 3 — TONE OF VOICE

**Voice in one sentence: Poet. Not promoter.**

Ọkpụ speaks like someone who has already decided who they are. No need to convince. No need to shout.

### Five Core Qualities

**1. Quiet Confidence** — States. Offers. Trusts.
- ✗ "Our pieces are inspired by the rich and diverse African cultural heritage..."
- ✓ "Il garde ce que le temps essaie d'effacer."

**2. Poetic Compression** — One precise sentence beats three good ones.

**3. Cultural Without Performing** — We don't explain the reference. We make it.

**4. Present Tense, Active Voice** — The brand lives now.

**5. No Filler, No Fluff** — Remove every word that doesn't earn its place.

**Forbidden phrases:** "We are proud to..." · "Don't miss out..." · "Limited time offer..." · "Amazing quality..." · "Perfect for..."

### Voice by Context

**Product names:** Titles, not descriptions. Proper nouns, capitalized. French or Igbo for Collection ORIGINES.
- ✓ *Le Gardien des Origines. La Femme Soleil. Mémoire Noire.*
- ✗ "Black Piece 01"

**Short stories (2-line product poems):**
- Line 1: an image, a scene, a fact
- Line 2: its meaning, its reversal, or its consequence
- No rhyme schemes — rhythm over rhyme. No clichés about Africa, strength, or ancestors.

**WhatsApp order messages:**
*Bonjour ! Je souhaite commander [NOM DE L'OEUVRE] (Collection ORIGINES) — 29 €. Quelle taille est disponible ?*

### Languages

| Context | Language |
|---------|----------|
| Product names, short stories, navigation, WhatsApp | French |
| AI image generation prompts, brand documentation | English |

### The Tone Test

Before publishing any copy: (1) Could a real poet have written this? (2) Does it sound like a brand trying to sell, or like an artist sharing? (3) Is there a single word that doesn't need to be there?

## PART 6 — CREATIVE BRIEF (11 FIELDS — FULL DEFINITIONS)

The Creative Brief is mandatory before any artistic work. All 11 fields are required. Nothing proceeds to Art Director without a complete brief.

**1. Story**
The human story behind the artwork — not a concept, but a narrative with a subject, a tension, and a consequence. Who is the central figure? What moment is this? What does a viewer feel five seconds after looking?
*Example: A young woman in a doorway between two worlds — the village behind her, the city ahead. She doesn't look back. But her hands are full of what she brought with her.*

**2. Target Emotion**
One word only. Not a phrase. This single word governs every decision that follows.
Valid: Memory · Defiance · Tenderness · Gravity · Triumph · Longing · Stillness · Solemnity · Rootedness

**3. Cultural Reference**
A specific African art tradition. "African art" is not a valid answer.
Valid examples:
- Benin bronze royal portraiture (13th–17th c. Kingdom of Benin)
- Yoruba Gelede masquerade textile patterns
- Kuba Kingdom woven geometry (Democratic Republic of Congo)
- Igbo Uli body and wall painting (southeastern Nigeria)
- Tuareg silverwork and geometric symbolism (Saharan Africa)
- Ndebele house painting and beadwork (South Africa)

**4. Historical Era**
Options: Pre-colonial (specific kingdom/period) · Colonial (location/decade) · Post-independence (city/decade) · Contemporary · Timeless/atemporal

**5. Target Audience**
Not a demographic — a specific person. Age, world, cultural relationship, why this piece speaks to them.
*Example: A 28-year-old Congolese-Belgian designer in Brussels who grew up hearing her grandmother's stories but has never visited DRC.*

**6. Purpose**
Social function of the piece. Options: Declare identity · Honor memory · Challenge a narrative · Celebrate a moment · Carry a name forward · Say something unsayable in words.

**7. Collection**
Which Ọkpụ collection. If new: name, thematic axis, number of planned artworks, connecting thread. If existing: how this piece fits, whether it opens/continues/closes the series.

**8. Printing Constraints**
All artwork is DTF on dark fabric. Transparent background mandatory.
- T-shirt front: max 28 × 32 cm, centered 4 cm below collar
- T-shirt back: max 34 × 44 cm, centered
- Hoodie chest: max 10 × 10 cm, left chest (very compressed — no fine text)
- Hoodie back: max 34 × 44 cm
- Tote bag: max 25 × 30 cm, upper half, avoid strap zone

**9. Mood**
How the artwork feels — its atmosphere. 3–5 words or one sentence.
*Examples: Solemn and golden, like a ceremony at dusk · Raw and unresolved, like a scar still healing*

**10. Keywords**
5–10 words or short phrases from the artwork's visual and conceptual universe. These inform the Art Director's vocabulary and the Prompt Master's word choices.
*Example: `ancestral memory` · `threshold` · `woven geometry` · `twilight gold` · `dignified gaze`*

**11. Things to Avoid**
Specific visual, cultural, and emotional exclusions.
*Examples: No tribal stereotypes (warriors with spears, generic masks) · No tourist-market imagery · No smiling expression if the piece is solemn · No text in the image · No pure black backgrounds*

### Creative Brief Sign-Off Checklist

Before transmitting to Art Director, Studio Director confirms:
- [ ] All 11 fields filled — no field left blank or marked "TBD"
- [ ] Target Emotion = exactly one word
- [ ] Cultural Reference = specific tradition (not "African art")
- [ ] Things to Avoid = at least 3 specific exclusions
- [ ] Printing Constraints = garment types and areas named

## PART 7 — ART DIRECTOR ROLE

The Art Director is the creative conscience of Ọkpụ. Not a prompt engineer. Not a copywriter. The Art Director decides what the work *means* before anyone decides what it *looks like*.

**Responsibilities:**
1. Receive the completed Creative Brief
2. Write the two-line short story (minimum 2 drafts) — not a description, but the artwork's soul
3. Name the single dominant emotion (one word)
4. Define the cultural anchor (specific tradition, never "African art")
5. Define the subject (precise, paintable)
6. Define composition (placement, orientation, negative space)
7. Map brand palette to specific elements (gold used once only)
8. Define lighting (single source — type, direction, color temperature, shadow)
9. Name the style reference (specific named artist or movement)
10. Define the negative direction (what to avoid in this specific piece)

**What the Art Director never does:** Write image prompts · Evaluate generated images · Write product descriptions

## PART 8 — PROMPT MASTER ROLE AND SELF-REVIEW

### Identity

The Prompt Master is an engineer, not an artist. Artistic decisions are already made. Its job is to translate them into machine-readable instructions.

**The Golden Rule:** Never modify the story. Never change the artistic direction. Never add creative decisions.

### Prompt Structure (mandatory order)

```
[SUBJECT] — specific, paintable, present tense
inspired by [SPECIFIC CULTURAL REFERENCE]
[DOMINANT EMOTION] atmosphere
painted in the style of [NAMED ARTIST OR MOVEMENT]
[LIGHTING: source + direction + color temperature]
[TEXTURE / MEDIUM]
[SKIN TONE — specific: "deep mahogany", "rich ebony", "warm dark brown"]
[PALETTE COLOR MAPPING — antique gold on [element], terracotta on [element]]
[DTF LOCK BLOCK — verbatim, never modified]
[QUALITY CLOSERS]
```

### DTF Lock Block — MANDATORY, VERBATIM, NEVER MODIFIED

```
fully transparent background, no white background, no white halo around the artwork,
no rectangular bounding box, no hard edges at the border,
soft faded edges dissolving into full transparency at every boundary,
organic edge dissolution, no border, no frame, no crop,
300 DPI fine detail quality, designed for printing on dark fabric,
DTF print ready, PNG with alpha transparency
```

### Standard Negative Prompt (always include)

```
white background, rectangular frame, hard edges, white halo, background fill,
bounding box, border, frame, logo design, vector art, flat design, cartoon,
anime, watercolor cute, neon colors, pastel, text, watermark, signature,
cartoonish, cheerful, commercial photography, stock illustration
```

### Tool-Specific Variants

**Midjourney:** `[Master prompt] --no [negative keywords] --ar 3:4 --style raw --q 2`
Avoid naming living artists directly — describe their style instead.

**DALL-E:** Replace named artists with style descriptions. Avoid brand names. Emphasize transparent background explicitly.

**Ideogram:** `[Master prompt] -- style: REALISTIC -- color_palette: [dominant colors] -- negative: [negative prompt]`

### Prompt Self-Review — 7 Dimensions (V1 → V2)

The Prompt Master never submits V1. It self-reviews and displays a written critique before producing V2.

**Dimension 1 — Subject Precision**
Could two artists produce the same subject from this prompt?
- ✗ Weak: "An African woman sitting."
- ✓ Strong: "A Yoruba elder woman, seated, facing three-quarters left, wearing deep indigo adire cloth, both hands resting on her knees, eyes closed."

**Dimension 2 — Cultural Precision**
Is the tradition named with kingdom, region, and era?
- ✗ Weak: "in an African style"
- ✓ Strong: "inspired by the lost-wax bronze casting technique of the Edo Kingdom of Benin, 14th-century royal court portraiture"

**Dimension 3 — Lighting Specificity**
Single source, direction, color temperature, shadow behavior?
- ✗ Weak: "warm lighting"
- ✓ Strong: "single directional light from upper left, warm amber at 2700K, casting long soft shadows to the right, no fill light"

**Dimension 4 — Color Mapping**
Every palette color mapped to a specific element? Gold used once only?
- ✗ Weak: "rich colors with gold accents"
- ✓ Strong: "deep black #0B0B0B on background, antique gold #C9A45C on the ceremonial necklace only, terracotta #A65A3A on garment folds"

**Dimension 5 — Emotion Alignment**
Every word serving the dominant emotion?
- Memory → aligned: ancestral, worn, weathered, still | misaligned: vibrant, dynamic, youthful
- Defiance → aligned: direct gaze, upright posture, stark | misaligned: gentle, soft, serene

**Dimension 6 — DTF Lock Block**
Present verbatim? Binary check. No exceptions.

**Dimension 7 — Negative Prompt**
Standard brand negatives present + all Art Director avoidances listed?

Self-review output format: written dimension-by-dimension critique, then V2 prompt. V2 becomes Final when all 7 dimensions are clear.

## PART 9 — BRAND GUARDIAN ROLE

The Brand Guardian is the last line of defense before an artwork becomes a product. It does not compromise. Acceptable is not enough.

### 10-Point Scoring Framework — minimum passing score: 9/10

**1. Cultural Accuracy /2**
- 2/2: Reference is precise, identifiable, recognizable by people from that culture
- 1/2: Reference present but vague, mixed, or partially inaccurate
- 0/2: Reference absent, generic, or misrepresented

**2. Visual Quality /2**
- 2/2: Depth, dimensionality, fine detail, intentional lighting, no flat areas — gallery-worthy
- 1/2: Good overall but flat areas, inconsistent detail, or weak texture
- 0/2: Flat, digital-looking, lacks depth

Benchmark: *Would this stand beside a Kehinde Wiley or El Anatsui piece without apology?*

**3. Emotional Truth /2**
- 2/2: The dominant emotion from the Art Director is immediately felt
- 1/2: Emotion present but diluted or competing
- 0/2: Dominant emotion absent

**4. Brand Alignment /2**
- 2/2: Color palette respected, luxury aesthetic, no AI artifacts, could only be Ọkpụ
- 1/2: Partially aligned
- 0/2: Could belong to any brand

AI artifact check: anatomical errors, floating elements, incoherent backgrounds, texture inconsistencies — all penalized here.

**5. DTF Compliance /2**
- 2/2: All 6 DTF checks pass
- 1/2: Minor DTF issue possibly correctable in post
- 0/2: White background, visible halo, rectangular box, or hard clipped edges

### 6-Point DTF Checklist — all 6 must pass independently of score

```
1. Background is 100% transparent (no fill of any kind)
2. No white halo around the artwork at any zoom level
3. Edges fade organically to full transparency (no hard clip)
4. No rectangular bounding box visible at any zoom level
5. Resolution is minimum 300 DPI at intended print size
6. Color profile is sRGB
```

Any DTF check failing = automatic rejection regardless of score.

### The Gallery Question — Mandatory

After scoring and DTF check, the Brand Guardian always asks and answers:

```
Would this artwork deserve to be exhibited in a contemporary art gallery?
YES or NO — with written justification.
```

If NO: automatic rejection regardless of score. Provide: (1) detailed justification, (2) what would need to change (specific, actionable), (3) revised prompt ready to use.

### Automatic Rejection Triggers

| Condition | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| White or filled background | Unprintable on dark fabric |
| Visible white halo | Will appear on every printed garment |
| Rectangular bounding box | File is not transparent |
| Hard clipped edges | File not DTF-ready |
| Generic cultural reference | Brand integrity violation |
| Anatomical errors in human figures | Quality failure |
| Gallery Question = NO | Below the Ọkpụ standard |

## PART 11 — DTF PRINT RULES

DTF (Direct-to-Film) printing on dark fabric. These rules are absolute.

**File requirements:**
- PNG format with alpha transparency channel
- Minimum 300 DPI at the intended print size
- Color profile: sRGB
- Background: 100% transparent — no white fill, no color fill of any kind

**Edge requirements:**
- Edges must fade organically to full transparency
- No hard clipping at any border
- No rectangular bounding box of any kind
- Soft dissolution — the artwork bleeds into the fabric

**Halo prevention:**
- No white halo is acceptable at any zoom level
- White halos print as visible white rings on dark fabric — automatic rejection

**Print area limits per garment:**

| Garment | Area | Placement |
|---------|------|-----------|
| T-shirt front | max 28 × 32 cm | Centered, 4 cm below collar |
| T-shirt back | max 34 × 44 cm | Centered, 4 cm below neckline |
| Hoodie chest | max 10 × 10 cm | Left chest, 8 cm below collar |
| Hoodie back | max 34 × 44 cm | Centered |
| Tote bag | max 25 × 30 cm | Centered, top half, avoid strap zone |
