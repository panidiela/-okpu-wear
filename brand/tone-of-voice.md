# Ọkpụ — Tone of Voice

> Ọkpụ speaks like someone who has already decided who they are.
> No need to convince. No need to shout.

> **→ Applied in production by:** [brand/system/05-role-product-writer.md](system/05-role-product-writer.md)
> **→ Enforced across all outputs by:** [brand/system/04-role-brand-guardian.md](system/04-role-brand-guardian.md)

## The Voice in One Sentence

**Poet. Not promoter.**

---

## Core Qualities

### 1. Quiet Confidence
Ọkpụ never explains itself defensively. It states. It offers. It trusts the audience to understand.

❌ *"Our bonnets are inspired by the rich and diverse African cultural heritage..."*
✅ *"Ọkpụ signifie bonnet. Ce mot porte ce que mille explications ne sauraient dire."*

---

### 2. Poetic Compression
Say more with less. One precise sentence beats three good ones.

❌ *"This piece is inspired by the concept of ancestral memory and the idea that our ancestors are always with us in spirit."*
✅ *"Il garde ce que le temps essaie d'effacer."*

---

### 3. Cultural Without Performing
Ọkpụ references African culture as something lived, not displayed. We don't explain the reference — we make it.

❌ *"In traditional Igbo culture, headwear holds deep spiritual significance..."*
✅ *"Ọkpụ. The word carries what the translation cannot."*

---

### 4. Present Tense, Active Voice
The brand lives now. Its references are ancient. Its language is present.

❌ *"This collection was inspired by the ancestral dances of West Africa."*
✅ *"The ancestors are still dancing. Close your eyes. The earth remembers."*

---

### 5. No Filler, No Fluff
Remove every word that doesn't earn its place.

Forbidden phrases:
- "We are proud to..."
- "Don't miss out..."
- "Limited time offer..."
- "Check out our..."
- "Amazing quality..."
- "Perfect for..."

---

## Voice by Context

### Product Names
Titles, not descriptions. They carry weight on their own.
*Le Gardien des Origines. La Femme Soleil. Mémoire Noire.*

Rules:
- Proper nouns, capitalized
- French or Igbo preferred for Collection ORIGINES
- No generic descriptions (not "Black Bonnet 01")

---

### Short Stories (2-line product poems)
The most intimate voice of the brand. Each product page carries a 2-line story.

Rules:
- Line 1: an image, a scene, a fact
- Line 2: its meaning, its reversal, or its consequence
- No rhyme schemes — rhythm over rhyme
- No clichés about Africa, strength, or ancestors

Examples:
> *Il garde ce que le temps essaie d'effacer. Porter ce bonnet, c'est choisir de ne pas oublier.*

> *Tu peux traverser l'océan. L'esprit du village, lui, ne quitte jamais tes épaules.*

---

### Navigation & UI
Minimal. Uppercase. Measured spacing.
- *Collection* not *Shop*
- *Découvrir l'œuvre* not *View product*
- *Commander sur WhatsApp* not *Buy now*
- *Notre histoire* not *About us*

---

### Social Media (future)
Short. Imagistic. Never a call to action.
- A quote from the product's short story
- A single image, no caption needed
- Or: one sentence. Period.

---

### WhatsApp (ordering)
Warm but precise. Pre-written messages stay formal enough to be taken seriously.

Template:
> *Bonjour ! Je souhaite commander [NOM DE L'ŒUVRE] (Collection ORIGINES) — 29 €. Quelle taille est disponible ?*

Rules:
- Greet before asking
- Name the product with its full title, in italics
- Price included — no ambiguity
- Ask, don't demand

## The Test

Before publishing any copy, ask:
1. Could a real poet have written this?
2. Does it sound like a brand trying to sell, or like an artist sharing?
3. Is there a single word that doesn't need to be there?

If you answer badly to any of these — rewrite.
