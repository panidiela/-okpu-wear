# Ọkpụ — Brand Guidelines

> The visual and creative rules that define how Ọkpụ looks, feels, and communicates.
> Every decision should pass this test: *does this feel like a gallery, or does it feel like a store?*

> **→ Creative production system:** [brand/system/00-overview.md](system/00-overview.md)
> **→ How these guidelines are enforced:** [brand/system/04-role-brand-guardian.md](system/04-role-brand-guardian.md)

---

## 1. Color Palette

These five colors are the entire palette. Nothing outside this system is permitted in primary brand materials.

| Name       | Hex       | Usage |
|------------|-----------|-------|
| Deep Black | `#0B0B0B` | Primary background, text, borders |
| Off-White  | `#F7F4EE` | Page backgrounds, light surfaces |
| Gold       | `#C9A45C` | Accents, CTAs, highlights, collection labels |
| Terra      | `#A65A3A` | Warm accents, secondary highlights, illustration tones |
| Soft Gray  | `#B7B2A9` | Secondary text, metadata, subtle UI elements |

### Usage Rules

- **Gold is scarce.** Use it to draw attention to the single most important element on a surface. Not three things — one.
- **Off-white, not white.** Pure white `#FFFFFF` never appears in brand materials. It reads as clinical, not cultural.
- **Deep black, not charcoal.** `#0B0B0B` has warmth. Pure black `#000000` does not.
- **Terra is for art, not UI.** Terra is reserved for illustration, pattern, and artwork contexts — not buttons or navigation.

## 2. Typography

### Display — Cormorant Garamond
Used for: product names, headlines, collection titles, pull quotes, the logo wordmark.

- Weight: Light (300) and Regular (400) only — never bold in display
- Style: Italic is permitted for poetic fragments and short stories
- Size: Large. When in doubt, go bigger.
- Tracking: Slightly negative at large sizes (`-0.02em`)

### Body — Inter
Used for: navigation, metadata, labels, descriptions, UI elements, legal text.

- Weight: Light (300) and Regular (400) — never bold except for product names in lists
- Size: Small and measured. The body text should feel like a whisper, not a shout.
- Tracking: Positive on uppercase labels (`0.16em` to `0.25em`)

### Rules

- **Never mix two display fonts.** Cormorant Garamond is the only serif.
- **All caps + wide tracking** is the Inter pattern for labels and navigation.
- **Never use font weights above 500** in primary brand contexts.

## 3. Logo

The wordmark is the single word: **Ọkpụ**

- Rendered in Cormorant Garamond, Light
- The diacritics (ọ and ụ) are non-negotiable — they are the name
- Tracking: `0.08em`
- No logomark, icon, or symbol replaces the wordmark at this stage
- Minimum size: 18px / 13pt

### Versions
| Version | Use |
|---------|-----|
| Default | Deep Black on Off-White backgrounds |
| Light | Off-White on Dark backgrounds |
| Gold | Gold on Deep Black — special/luxury contexts only |

## 4. Spacing & Layout

Ọkpụ layouts breathe. The absence of content is as intentional as the content itself.

- **Generous white space.** More than you think. Then double it.
- **Single focal point per screen section.** Never two competing elements at the same visual weight.
- **Asymmetry is permitted.** Rigid grids feel corporate. Tension and balance coexist.
- **Section padding:** minimum `6rem` vertical on desktop, `4rem` on mobile.

## 5. Imagery

### Photography (when used)
- Natural light only — no studio flash aesthetics
- Skin tones rendered accurately, never brightened or normalized
- Background: neutral (off-white, stone, black) — never colorful
- Framing: close, intimate — the product and the person, nothing else

### Artwork / Illustrations
- Inspired by: West African sculpture, Igbo geometric motifs, kente patterns, adinkra symbols, Benin bronzes
- Style: contemporary reinterpretation — never pastiche, never costume
- Color: drawn from the brand palette only
- No clip art, no stock vectors, no generic "African" pattern packs

### DTF Print Artwork (see `prompts/dtf-rules.md` for full spec)
- No white backgrounds or rectangular bounding boxes
- Faded edges — the art bleeds into the fabric
- Gallery quality — not streetwear graphic, not illustration for a children's book
- Fine detail capable of 300 DPI print reproduction

---

## 6. Tone (summary — full version in `tone-of-voice.md`)

- Speaks like a poet, not a salesperson
- Direct, not verbose
- References culture without explaining it
- Never apologetic about what it is

---

## 7. What to Avoid

| Avoid | Because |
|-------|---------|
| Neon or saturated colors | Contradicts the palette system |
| Drop shadows on text | Cheap, dated |
| Emojis in brand copy | Breaks the tone |
| Exclamation marks | Desperation, not confidence |
| "African-inspired" as a descriptor | Vague, reductive |
| White halos on DTF prints | Amateur print quality |
| Rounded corners on product cards | Feels e-commerce, not gallery |
