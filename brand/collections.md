## How Collections Work

- Each collection has a **number** (001, 002...) and a **name** (ORIGINES, etc.)
- Collections are **limited** — no restocks after sell-out
- Each collection is built around a **cultural anchor**: a concept, a people, a moment in time
- Collections may share the same garment type (bonnets) but must have distinct visual and narrative identities

## Collection 001 — ORIGINES

**Status:** Active — Released June 2026
**Pieces:** 10
**Garment:** Bonnet (cap)
**Price:** 29 €
**Language:** French

### Creative Anchor
The origin. Not as mythology — as lived memory. ORIGINES explores what it means to come from somewhere specific, and to carry that somewhere in your body, your language, your way of moving through the world.

### Visual Direction
- Palette: Deep black + off-white + gold accents
- References: Igbo sculpture, Yoruba bronze casting, kente geometry
- Mood: Solemn, grounded, luminous

### Naming Convention
Proper nouns with articles. French. Characters and archetypes, not descriptions.
Examples: *Le Gardien des Origines, La Femme Soleil, Mémoire Noire*

## Naming Rules (Cross-Collection)

1. Names are **artworks titles**, not product names
2. They carry their own meaning without explanation
3. They must resist translation — the name in its original language is the name
4. No numbered variants (not "Bonnet 01 noir") — each piece is unique
5. No color in the name — a piece is not named by what it looks like

---

## Archive Policy

When a collection sells out:
- The collection page remains online as an archive
- Products become `available: false`
- No restock, no reproduction
- A collection archive badge is added: *Collection terminée — Sold out*
