## The Name

**Ọkpụ** (pronounced *okpu*) means *hat* or *cap* in Igbo, a language spoken primarily in southeastern Nigeria and across the African diaspora. The word carries cultural weight far beyond its translation — headwear in many African traditions is a mark of dignity, identity, and spiritual connection.

We chose this word deliberately. It is African in origin, difficult to domesticate, and impossible to reduce. It demands to be learned.

---

## Why We Exist

African cultural identity is too often flattened — reduced to patterns, prints, and aesthetics stripped of meaning. Ọkpụ exists to reverse that.

We create wearable pieces rooted in African artistic heritage — not as nostalgia, but as a living, evolving practice. Each piece in a collection is an artwork first. The garment is its carrier.

We exist for:
- Those who carry their roots with pride and complexity
- The diaspora navigating between worlds
- Anyone who believes that what you wear can say something true

---

## The Core Belief

**Art does not belong in museums alone.**

A great painting on a wall reaches a few hundred people a year. A great design on a cap worn daily reaches thousands — on streets, in classrooms, in airports, in the mirror every morning.

Ọkpụ treats streetwear as a gallery without walls.

---

## The Long Vision

To build the most culturally significant African wearable art brand of its generation.

Not the biggest. Not the loudest. The most meaningful.

In ten years, when someone picks up an Ọkpụ piece from Collection 001, they should feel the same thing they feel holding a first edition — the weight of something that was made with intention, released at the right moment, and that will only grow in value.

---

## What We Are Not

- We are not a streetwear brand with African aesthetics bolted on
- We are not a heritage brand that romanticizes the past
- We are not a charity or a cause — we are a creative practice
- We are not mass market, and we will never pretend to be

---

## The Promise

Every Ọkpụ piece is:

1. **Rooted** — grounded in a specific cultural reference, not vague Afro-aesthetic
2. **Intentional** — every element of the design has a reason
3. **Limited** — we do not overproduce
4. **Wearable** — art that lives on the body, not behind glass
