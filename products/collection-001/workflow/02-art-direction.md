## 1. Subject Description

What is literally in the image. Be specific about every element.

```
Who or what is the central subject:
______________________________________________________________

Pose / gesture / action:
______________________________________________________________

What they wear or hold:
______________________________________________________________

Relationship to the viewer (frontal / profile / back / aerial / abstract):
______________________________________________________________

Secondary elements (background figures, objects, nature):
______________________________________________________________
```

**Good subject descriptions:**
- *"A stoic male figure in profile, wearing a crown woven from ancient roots and bones, hands resting on a carved wooden staff, eyes closed in meditation"*
- *"A radiant woman with her eyes turned upward, arms slightly raised as if receiving light, hair adorned with golden kente thread and small sunbursts"*

**Bad subject descriptions:**
- *"An African man with a hat"*
- *"A powerful woman"*

## 3. Lighting

**Rule:** One dominant light source. Direction matters. Name it precisely.

```
Light source type:   ______________________________________________
Direction:           ______________________________________________
Color of light:      ______________________________________________
Shadow behavior:     ______________________________________________
```

**Options:**
- *"Warm golden rim light from upper left — the only light source, deep shadow on the right"*
- *"Light emerging from within the figure — no external source visible"*
- *"Single overhead spotlight, deep vertical shadows in the carved features"*
- *"Low horizon light, amber and warm — candlelight quality from below the face"*
- *"Diffuse golden hour light from the left, long shadows extending right"*


**AI prompt vocabulary:**
- Gold → *"antique gold", "burnished brass", "aged metallic"*
- Terra → *"terracotta", "red earth", "burnt sienna", "laterite"*
- Black → *"obsidian", "deep matte black", "carbon void"*

---

## 5. Texture & Medium

The surface quality of the artwork. Determines the feel — painterly vs. sculptural vs. photographic.

```
Primary medium / texture:   ___________________________________
Secondary texture detail:   ___________________________________
```

**Options:**
- *"Oil on aged linen canvas — visible brushwork in shadows"*
- *"Dark bronze casting — fine engraved surface detail"*
- *"Ink wash on heavily textured watercolor paper"*
- *"Hammered copper and woven textile collage"*
- *"Encaustic wax over oil — translucent layered depth"*

---

## 6. Art Style Reference

Name the specific artists or movements this piece borrows from visually.

```
Primary reference:   ______________________________________________
Secondary:           ______________________________________________
```

**Approved references:**
- Kehinde Wiley — baroque portraiture of Black subjects
- El Anatsui — woven metallic assemblage
- Kerry James Marshall — deep black as subject, not shadow
- Wangechi Mutu — mythological collage, body and nature
- Seydou Keïta — formal studio portraiture, West African
- Baroque oil (Rembrandt, Caravaggio) — chiaroscuro applied to African subjects
- Byzantine iconography — flat gold ground, frontal gaze, sacred
