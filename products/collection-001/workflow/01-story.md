## Step 1 · The Short Story

The two-line poetic story that will appear on the product page.

**Rules:**
- Line 1 is an image, a scene, or a fact — concrete and specific
- Line 2 is its meaning, its reversal, or its consequence
- No rhyme schemes — rhythm over rhyme
- No clichés about Africa, strength, or ancestors
- If you can replace a word with a more precise one — replace it

## Step 2 · The Dominant Emotion

Name the **single** emotion this piece must produce in the viewer.
Not two. Not "powerful and tender." One.

```
Dominant emotion: _____________________________________________

In one sentence, why this emotion:
______________________________________________________________
______________________________________________________________
```

**Emotion palette — choose one:**

| Emotion | What it means in this context |
|---------|-------------------------------|
| Solemnity | The weight of something sacred |
| Luminosity | Light that comes from within, not from outside |
| Grief | Not sorrow — grief that has been transformed into beauty |
| Pride | Not performance — the quiet certainty of knowing who you are |
| Rootedness | The feeling of being held by something older than yourself |
| Longing | Distance from origin — diaspora emotion |
| Power | The kind that doesn't need to announce itself |
| Serenity | Stillness after the storm, not before |
| Defiance | Unbroken — presence in spite of erasure |


**Reference Library — quick pick:**

| Tradition | Visual Key | Use for |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Benin bronzes (Edo, Nigeria) | Bronze relief, regal portraiture | Royalty, power, permanence |
| Yoruba Egungun (Nigeria) | Masquerade, layered costume | Ancestors, ceremonial, transformation |
| Igbo Ukwu metalwork (Nigeria) | Intricate geometric surface | Complexity, craft, origin |
| Dogon sculpture (Mali) | Elongated forms, cosmic alignment | Timelessness, sky, afterlife |
| Kuba Kingdom textiles (DRC) | Woven geometry, pattern maps | Village, structure, homeland |
| Dahomey Agojie (Benin Rep.) | Female warrior, red earth | Queens, resistance, erased power |
| Kente (Ghana/Ivory Coast) | Interlocking color strips | Rhythm, identity, celebration |
| Adinkra (Ghana) | Symbolic stamps, philosophical | Meaning, proverb, wisdom |
| Ethiopian iconography | Gold leaf, Byzantine-African | Sacred, divine, gold |
